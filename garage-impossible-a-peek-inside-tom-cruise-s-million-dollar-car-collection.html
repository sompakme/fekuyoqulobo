Tom Cruise is one of the biggest action stars globally and has become an icon with his high-profile movies since the 1980s. He rose to prominence after his starring role in Top Gun. Cruise is known for his dedicated stunt scenes and action sequences that the actor prefers to do himself most of the time. He has been a part of hit franchises like Mission Impossible and Jack Reacher that has heightened his love for vintage and fast cars. With a net worth of $600 million, he is one of the highest-paid actors in the world. Cruise spends his money stocking his garage with the cars on his movie sets and also has some personal vintage purchases.

From the American classics in the late 1940s to sleek and modern German engineered vehicles, Tom Cruise has many cars from memorable movies. Let’s take a look at his multi-million dollar car collection.

Buick Roadmaster (1949)

Tom Cruise starred in the 1988 classic Rain Man alongside Dustin Hoffman and filmed some shots of the movie driving the 1949 Buick Roadmaster. The actor loved the car and used it to cruise through many cross-country road trips. The car driven by Cruise and Hoffman was sold at an auction for $170,500.

Chevrolet Corvette C1 (1958)

A beloved car in Cruise’s garage, Chevrolet struggled with sales when it decided to introduce the Corvette, an American sports classic. The production for Corvettes began in 1953, and for the 1958 model, the C1 came with a dual headlight and updated interior, as noted by Car and Driver. The original price of the C1 was $3,591.

Chevrolet Chevelle SS (1970)

Cruise gave his Chevelle Super Sport a starring role in the 2012 movie Jack Reacher. The SS gives an output of 350 HP with a power bulge under the hood. The Chevelle gained popularity in the Nascar circuit during the 1970s as a powerful muscle car. While the car may have had a reasonable price during the 70s today, the car is worth $72,900.

Dodge Colt (1976)

The first car in Cruise’s collection was a $925 1976 Dodge Colt that he purchased in high school. Growing up in a modest and abusive home, the actor wanted to escape to New York to pursue his dream of becoming an actor. The 1.6-liter compact engine helped him reach the city of New York, where he slowly gained movie roles that made him a star.

Porsche 928 (1979)

Another car that Cruise fell in love with on the movie set, he purchased the 1979 Porsche 928 after filming his 1983 breakthrough movie Risky Business. A luxurious car, the 928 is the only car by Porsche to have a front-mounted V8 engine. According to GQ, the movie car was sold for upwards of $40,000 at an auction, and Cruise purchased a new one for himself.

BMW 3-Series E30 (1983)

A symbol of ambition and new technology, Cruise purchased the BMW 3-series E30 after his supporting roles in the movies The Outsiders and Taps. It was the first BMW car in his collection and cost $6,500.

Nissan 300ZX SCCA Race Car (1988)

Cruise turned his hand towards the world of racing when he participated in the Sports Car Club of America Championship. With Paul Newman as the mentor, he won several races and crashed a few. The original Nissan 300ZX costs upwards of $36,000.

Porsche 993 (1996)

In the movie Risky Business, Tom Cruise said there is no substitute for Porsche, and the actor stood true to his words. He preferred the 993 over the 911s, which came with a cosmetic update and cost $63,750. As mentioned by Top Speed, it was one of the last Porsches to be air-cooled.

Ford Excursion (2000)

The tank-like Ford Excursion has helped Cruise stay out of the paparazzi radar as he uses the car as his decoy. When she was pregnant, the Church of Scientology custom-built the Excursion to protect his then-wife, Katie Holmes.

Bugatti Veyron (2005)

Tom Cruise bought the Veyron for $1.7 million in 2005, the same year that the car hit the showrooms. The best of the Bugatti, the Veyron is equipped with a W16 8.0-liter engine that gives nearly 1,000 HP. The actor and Katie Holmes had arrived on the red carpet of Mission Impossible: III in the same car.

Saleen Mustang S281 (2010)

A Made in America shredded car, the Saleen Mustang S281 breathes the V8 by Ford. Cruise uses the $52,000 car to run errands and drive down the streets of Los Angeles.

BMW 7 Series (2015)

After the Mission Impossible franchise partnered with BMW, Cruise is always driving one of its cars, as stated by Hot Cars. BMW gifted him their top-of-the-line seven series that he used during the press tour, movie promotions, and in the movies.

Tom Cruise not only has an impeccable car collection, but the actor is also a bike lover who has several high-end models in his garage. An automobile enthusiast, Tom Cruise is one of those actors who is as cool on-screen as off-screen. He will next be seen starring in Top Gun: Maverick and in chase scenes in a BMW for his future Mission Impossible installments.
