Julie Bowen Biography and Wiki

Julie Bowen is an American actress who was born on March 3, 1970 in Baltimore, Maryland, USA. She is best known for playing Claire Dunphy on the TV comedy series Modern Family.

She also played Aunt Gwen on Dawson’s Creek, Carol Vessey on Ed, Denise Bauer on Boston Legal, and Sarah Shephard on Lost. Her Modern Family role brought her six nominations for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series, which she won in 2011 and 2012.

The show contains elements of supernatural and science fiction and follows the survivors of a commercial jet airliner flying between Sydney and Los Angeles after the plane crashes on a mysterious island somewhere in the South Pacific Ocean.

Julie Bowen Happy Gilmore

Bowen appeared on Happy Gilmore,  an American sports comedy film of 1998 as Virginia Venit. The comedy film was directed by Dennis Dugan, produced by Robert Simonds casting Adam Sandler as the title character, an unsuccessful ice hockey player who discovers a newfound talent for golf.

The screenplay was written by Sandler and his frequent collaborator Tim Herlihy, in their second feature collaboration after the previous year’s Billy Madison; the film also marks the first of multiple collaborations between Sandler and Dugan. The film was released in cinemas on February 16, 1996 by Universal Pictures.
