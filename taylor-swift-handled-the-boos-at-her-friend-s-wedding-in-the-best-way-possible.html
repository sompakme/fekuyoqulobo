Taylor Swift Handled The Boos At Her Friend's Wedding In The Best Way Possible

Publish date: 2024-09-14

Highlights

  • Taylor Swift faced boos from her own fans for avoiding them at a wedding, but we can't blame her for prioritizing her friend's special day.
  • Swift was booed by rival NFL fans when her ad played at MetLife Stadium, but she isn't bothered by the hate and remains unfazed.
  • Swift knows how to deal with negative publicity by avoiding articles that won't contribute positively to her life and not letting internet criticism affect her.

At the moment, Taylor Swift is on top of the music world. She's adding to her $1.1 billion net worth, and that'll only continue for years to come.

However, despite all the fame and fortune, Swift has faced lots of tough situations in the past, and even boos from her own fans. We'll take a closer look at why Swift was booed during her close friend's wedding, and why Swift decided against acknowledging her fans.

We're also going to take a closer look at another recent situation that saw Swift get booed, along with her thoughts on receiving hate.

Taylor Swift Was Booed At A Wedding After She Didn't Greet Fans Walking Out Of The Church

Taylor Swift was a bridesmaid a few years back at the wedding of close friend, Abigail Anderson. Clearly, Swift wanted all the attention to be on her close friend during the a special day. Therefore, she did her best to avoid any type of distractions.

However, that all changed when fans caught wind of Swift at the church after it was leaked on social media. Fans swarmed the church, and ultimately Taylor Swift was booed for avoiding fans.

TMZ writes, "Taylor Swift outraged a gaggle of fans Saturday who waited to catch of glimpse of her after a Martha's Vineyard wedding."

"TMZ got this video of Taylor's exit from the church ... where security went to great lengths to shield her from a bunch of kids and adults standing outside, hoping Tay Tay would grace them with her presence for just a moment. It was only a 10-foot walk from the church door to the waiting SUV, but Taylor didn't want to play ball, and the fans went from "We love you Taylor" to a chorus of boos, with some chanting "Very disappointing, not cool."

Although Swift was booed for the moment, we really can't blame her for not acknowledging the fans in this particular situation. We all know how great Swift is with her fans, but this wasn't the time and place to greet fans. Taylor was clearly trying to be a good friend, and put all her attention on the wedding instead.

Taylor Swift Is Still Getting Booed In Massive Stadiums But This Time By Football Fans

Given her involvement with Travis Kelce and the Chiefs, Taylor Swift isn't popular with rival fans in the NFL. That was deemed to be the case in New York when a Taylor Swift ad was played at MetLife Stadium. The Giants fans booed loudly at the Taylor Swift ad.

According to Sports Illustrated, fans reacted negatively to the moment.

"New York Giants fans don’t seem to be all that excited about the Taylor Swift craze sweeping the National Football League. Just 24 hours after Swift watched the Chiefs beat the Jets from a suite at MetLife Stadium, Giants fans in the same venue booed when an advertisement for Swift’s upcoming movie documenting her wildly popular Eras Tour was aired on the stadium jumbotron."

Without a doubt, we assume this trend among NFL fans will only continue, especially when Swift is at other locations outside the Chiefs' home field. Nonetheless, Swift isn't bothered by the hate, and her ways of dealing with it behind the scenes.

Taylor Swift Has Revealed How She Deals With Negative Publicity

Seventeen discussed the perfect responses Taylor Swift has given for haters. Swift spoke specifically about negative stories surfacing on the internet.

"I know when not to read an article. Is it going to help my day? Is it important for my life? If the answer is no, then I just don't click. I'm careful about getting sucked into the rabbit hole that is the Internet because, as a songwriter, I don't have the option of having thick skin. As a writer you have to be open to everything, and that includes pain, rejection, self-doubt, fear. I deal with that enough on my own. If you look hard enough, you could find somebody on the Internet criticizing every single thing about you. If you're me."

Swift would also discuss the common criticism she faces writing about her past relationships in songs. Once again, Swift doesn't read into the drama.

"For a female to write about her feelings, and then be portrayed as some clingy, insane, desperate girlfriend in need of making you marry her and have kids with her, I think that's taking something that potentially should be celebrated—a woman writing about her feelings in a confessional way—that's taking it and turning it and twisting it into something that is frankly a little sexist."

Despite the ups and downs, Swift has been able to deal with all the drama in the best way possible. She's currently regarded among the top artists in the world, if not, the very best. Her net worth has hit the $1 billion mark.
