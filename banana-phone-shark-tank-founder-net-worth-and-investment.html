Who Is The Founder Of Banana Phone?

The founders of Banana Phone are Brian Brunsing, Charlie Katrycz, and Max Brown. Brian, a marketing expert, is one of the pioneers behind Banana Phones. Max, the company’s CEO, also holds the position of the president at Garrett Leather Corporation. Charlie, on the other hand, is the CTO and the founder of Loonskin Labs. Together, they came together and established the Banana Phone company in 2017.

Banana Phone Before Shark Tank

Before appearing on Shark Tank, Banana Phone embarked on a successful crowdfunding campaign on Indiegogo in 2017. The campaign aimed to raise funds for product development and generate pre-orders for the unique Bluetooth handset.

Through the Indiegogo campaign, Banana Phone managed to raise an impressive $44,850, with the support of 530 backers. This financial boost allowed the company to bring their innovative product closer to reality.

The crowdfunding campaign was not only a financial success, but it also demonstrated the widespread appeal of the Banana Phone. The company received over 1000 units of pre-orders from 18 countries, attesting to the global interest in this quirky and entertaining gadget.

Furthermore, Banana Phone has made philanthropy an integral part of their business model. By pledging to donate 1% of their sales to Gearing Up 4 Gorillas, a non-profit organization dedicated to conserving mountain gorillas, Banana Phone contributes to the protection of these endangered species.

With the Indiegogo campaign and their commitment to conservation, Banana Phone laid the foundation for their venture, enabling them to move forward with confidence and bring their product to a wider audience.

How Was The Shark Tank Pitch Of Banana Phone?

During their Shark Tank pitch, the founders of Banana Phone made a compelling case for their product and its potential in the market. They asked for a $250,000 investment in exchange for a 10% equity stake in their company, which valued Banana Phone at $2.5 million.

With enthusiasm, they showcased their unique Bluetooth handset designed in the shape of a banana. The founders highlighted the entertainment value and the fun aspect of the product, emphasizing its potential as a conversation starter and a unique phone gadget.

Accompanying their pitch, the founders shared impressive sales figures that reflected the popularity of Banana Phone. They disclosed lifetime sales of $1.6 million, demonstrating a strong market demand for their product. Furthermore, the company had achieved a net income of $123,000 in the last 12 months.

Despite their compelling presentation and impressive sales figures, none of the Sharks were convinced to make an investment deal with Banana Phone. The Shark Tank investors evaluated the pitch, considering factors such as the product valuation, market potential, and the unique nature of the product. While they acknowledged the success of Banana Phone, the Sharks ultimately decided not to move forward with an investment deal.

“Banana Phone had an interesting pitch, and their sales figures were impressive. However, we had concerns about the sustainability of the product’s novelty and its long-term market potential. It was a tough decision not to invest, but we had to consider the overall viability of the opportunity.” – Shark Tank Investor

The Shark Tank pitch of Banana Phone generated a range of reactions from the Sharks, showcasing their individual preferences and investment strategies. Some Sharks saw potential in the product but hesitated due to market saturation concerns, while others questioned the scalability of a novelty phone gadget.

While Banana Phone did not secure an investment deal on Shark Tank, their pitch brought attention to their brand and product, boosting their visibility in the market. The founders left the tank determined to continue growing their business and capitalize on the momentum gained from appearing on the show.
