Cypress Hill’s Discography

Cypress Hill, with their unique blend of rap and rock influences, has released several successful albums throughout their career. Their discography showcases their evolution as artists and the impact they have had on the music industry.

  • Cypress Hill (1991): This self-titled debut album introduced the world to Cypress Hill’s iconic sound. It features hits like “How I Could Just Kill a Man” and “The Phuncky Feel One”. The album received critical acclaim and set the stage for their future success.
  • Black Sunday (1993): Considered one of their most significant albums, “Black Sunday” elevated Cypress Hill’s popularity to new heights. The album includes chart-topping singles like “Insane in the Brain” and “I Ain’t Goin’ Out Like That”. It became their first album to reach number one on the Billboard 200 chart.
  • III: Temples of Boom (1995): This album showcased a darker and more introspective side of Cypress Hill. With tracks like “Throw Your Set in the Air” and “Illusions”, they explored themes of spirituality and personal struggles. The album further solidified their reputation as innovators in the rap genre.
  • Rise Up (2010): After a hiatus, Cypress Hill returned with “Rise Up”, their eighth studio album. Collaborating with artists like Tom Morello and Marc Anthony, they brought fresh energy to their music. The album features the popular single “Armada Latina” and continues to showcase Cypress Hill’s versatility and enduring appeal.
