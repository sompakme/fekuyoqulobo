Thai businessman and politician, Thaksin Shinawatra, has a net worth of $1.86 billion. Thailand's former prime minister was ousted in 2006 coup, lives in self-imposed exile to avoid 2-year jail term after conviction in absentia on corruption charges. Thai authorities froze $2 billion in assets, largely from family's sale of Shin Corp., the telecom company he founded. He bought Britain's Manchester City football club in 2007.

His younger sister, Yingluck Shinawatra is Thailand's first female and current prime minister. Informally advises younger sister Yingluck, who became prime minister after a landslide victory in July polling. After Yingluck took office, finance ministry acknowledged it would not appeal a tax ruling that would release $40 million in frozen assets held by Thaksin's son and daughter. He currently lives in Dubai.
