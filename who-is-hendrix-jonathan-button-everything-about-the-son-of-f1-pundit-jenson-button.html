Jenson Button Wikipedia

Jenson Button was introduced to racing as a young boy by his father, John Button, a former rally-competition rider famous for racing a Volkswagon type 1.

Jenson loved driving a BMX bike as a kid and watching Formula One with his father.

His press coverage of F1’s career and personal life varied from minor to extensive. This effect has been marked ‘Buttonmania.’

Prior to winning the 2009 championship, Jenson’s lack of success prompted critics to call him “almost a man” and “a lightweight pin-up” because of his attractive appearance However, it ended with his victory.

At the 2010 New Year Honors for Motorsport services, he was appointed Member of the Order of the British Empire (MBE). Button’s home town, Frome, has named the street and footbridge carrying the River Frome after him.

Jenson was awarded the freedom of the town and he also recieved the award for honorary engineering doctorate in December 2016 by the University of Bath.

He has authored five books about his life and career.

Jenson’s hobbies include mountain biking, competing in triathlons, bodyboarding. He also maintains a car collection.
