Tupac Shakur, one of the most influential hip-hop artists of all time, left an indelible mark on the world through his music, poetry, and acting. Throughout his career, he released some hit songs that are still popular with fans today. Among these, his top-grossing songs are some of his most successful and memorable tracks. "California Love," a collaboration with Dr. Dre and Roger Troutman, peaked at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and sold over five million copies worldwide.

Tupac was known for his activism and outspokenness on issues such as police brutality, racism, and poverty. He was involved in several high-profile controversies, including his feud with fellow rapper The Notorious B.I.G. and his run-ins with the law. Despite his untimely death, Tupac's legacy lives on through his music, which inspires and influences artists and fans worldwide. Hit 'Em Up

Hit 'Em Up, a diss track aimed at the East Coast rap scene and some of its most well-known artists, is the eighth best-selling song by Tupac Shakur. The song did well in the marketplace, selling over a million copies worldwide. Even though Hit 'Em Up was never an official single, it is still a fan favorite and a great example of Tupac's lyrical skill and fearlessness. Tupac's reputation as a master of diss tracks was cemented by the song's angry tone and scathing lyrics, and it remains a highlight of his long career.

All Eyez on Me

All Eyez on Me, the title track of Tupac Shakur's 1996 album of the same name. The song, which has sold over one million copies worldwide, features a sample of Linda Clifford's disco hit Gypsy Lady and showcases Tupac's signature style of combining socially conscious lyrics with catchy beats. The album, released during the height of Tupac's career, has since become a classic in hip-hop and cemented his place as one of the genre's most iconic figures.

Dear Mama

Dear Mama is one of Tupac Shakur's most famous and emotional songs. It is a tribute to his mother, Afeni Shakur, and her struggles while raising him. When the song came out in 1995, it was a big hit right away, and it has since become a classic in the hip-hop world. It's one of Tupac's best songs and has sold over a million copies worldwide. Tupac's powerful delivery and the song's heartfelt lyrics make it a touching and deeply personal tribute to his mother. It also makes you think about the importance of family and the sacrifices parents make for their children.

2 of Amerikaz's Most Wanted

Tupac's collaboration with fellow West Coast rapper Snoop Dogg, 2 of Amerikaz's Most Wanted. The song featured the two legendary rappers' chemistry and lyrical skill, was released in 1996, and immediately became a hit. The movie has more than a million copies sold worldwide. 2 of Amerikaz Most Wanted is a testament to Tupac's lasting influence on the hip-hop scene and his ability to work with other artists to create memorable music, thanks to its infectious beat and catchy chorus.

I Ain't Mad At Cha

I Ain't Mad At Cha, a single from Tupac Shakur's album All Eyez on Me, comes in at four on the list of his highest-earning songs. The song, which features vocals from Danny Boy, is about losing a friend and moving past regrets. It was released in 1996 and quickly became a commercial success, selling more than a million copies globally. Fans connected with the soulful melody and Tupac's reflective lyrics, which helped him become known as a versatile and multifaceted artist. One of Tupac's most well-known and enduring songs, I Ain't Mad At Cha, is evidence of his artistry and lyrical depth.

Changes

Changes, a posthumously released track that has become one of Tupac's most enduring and influential songs, is ranked third on the list of Tupac's highest-grossing songs. The song, which features a sample of Bruce Hornsby's The Way It Is, was released in 1998, two years after Tupac's death. It has sold over two million copies worldwide and received critical acclaim for its socially conscious lyrics and commentary on poverty, racism, and police brutality. Changes is still a powerful and relevant social justice anthem, and its popularity has grown since its release, cementing Tupac's legacy as a voice for the oppressed and marginalized.

How Do U Want It

How Do U Want It, from the 1996 album All Eyez on Me, is one of Tupac's songs with the most significant sales. The song with K-Ci & JoJo topped the Billboard Hot 100 and sold over two million copies worldwide. Partygoers and West Coast rap fans loved the song's catchy chorus, fast tempo, and explicit lyrics. How Do U Want It cemented Tupac's status as a legendary hip-hop artist.

California Love

California Love by Tupac, was released in 1996 and features Dr. Dre and Roger Troutman, has generated the most earnings. The song was a big hit right away, going to the top of the charts in the U.S. and selling more than five million copies worldwide. California Love is a famous hip-hop song from the West Coast known for its catchy hook and beat. The song's music video, set in a post-apocalyptic world like Mad Max, made it even more of a cultural touchstone. California Love is still one of Tupac's most well-known and long-lasting songs.
